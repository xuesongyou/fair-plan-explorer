The capital buffer is extra money the insurer must hold above expected losses to survive a 1-in-100 bad year. Concentrated portfolios hold fewer, larger policies — even with zero correlation the binomial variance is far higher relative to the mean, forcing a much wider loss distribution and larger buffer. Adding correlation amplifies this further.
